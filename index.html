A day in the life of a reforestation volunteer in Brazil.This summer, our own Ashley
traveled to the spectacularly mountainous to take part in the expedition Wildlife
and Reforestation in Brazil.

  • Waking up in the midst of nature in the morning. I'm not a morning person
    in any sense, but the best time to spot wildlife is at dawn and dusk. And
    I'm not about to waste any sunrises when I have the rare opportunity to
    visit one of the world's most beautiful place.

  • Stepping onto the lush trails. Several trails snake through the grounds of, all
    of which are available to volunteers during their free time. There have been
    wildlife spotting on all of them, including capybara (the world's largest
    rodent), tapirs, and a plethora of birds.

  • Morning in the mountain mist. I quietly walk up the metal spiral stairs to the
    top of the lookout tower, trying not to make any loud noises that'd scare
    away nearby wildlife. Once I get to the top, I see beautiful
    view in the mountains.
